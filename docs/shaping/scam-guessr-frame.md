---

## Problem

Telejobs has a working fraud detection engine (SUS) and 24K+ job postings, but zero retention. Job searching is episodic — users check once when they need it, get value, and leave. There is no mechanism that brings them back on days when they are not actively job-hunting. The "crusader" framing (report fraud, protect others) produced a one-week engagement spike then collapsed — civic duty is not a daily habit.

The product needs a pull mechanism that is intrinsically rewarding independent of whether the user is currently job-hunting.

---

## Outcome

Prove D7 retention ≥ 30% within 4 weeks of launch. If not achieved, project closes.

Secondary: the mechanism that drives retention should also generate disagreement signals that improve the SUS model over time (data flywheel).
