# Persona Brief: Outraged Олег

## Identity
- **Name:** Олег Мартынов
- **Age:** 34
- **City:** Казань
- **Status:** Sysadmin at a mid-sized logistics company. Married, one kid (4 years old). Side hobby: OSINT and anti-fraud.

## A day in his life
Up at 7, coffee, checks work alerts, then personal Telegram before the kid wakes up. Subscribes to 30+ channels: security news, OSINT tools, anti-fraud, local Kazan news, few IT channels. Work is routine and slightly boring — he's good at it but it doesn't challenge him. The fraud-hunting hobby started 3 years ago when his cousin lost 40,000 rubles to a fake job scheme. Олег found the scammer's Telegram account, traced it to a network of 6 channels, reported all of them. Three got banned. That felt extraordinary.

Evening: after kid's bedtime (21:00), he has 1-2 hours. This is when he opens anti-fraud channels, hunts, sometimes posts findings to a chat of ~200 similar hobbyists.

## Relationship with technology
- Сompetent sysadmin: comfortable with CLI, basic Python scripts, Maltego for OSINT graphs
- Uses telejobs-style tools analytically: sorts, filters, looks for patterns
- Doesn't play games. Last "game" was chess on Lichess 2 years ago
- Cares about accuracy and evidence — dislikes tools that are vague or unverifiable
- Shares findings as structured posts with screenshots, not casual shares

## Emotional profile
- **Competence-driven:** Wants to be right and demonstrably right. The cousin incident gave him a mission.
- **Skeptical of automation:** "Algorithms miss context. I see things it doesn't."
- **Impact-hungry:** Reporting to admins who never respond is demoralizing. Wants feedback loops.
- **Low tolerance for gamification:** Badges and streaks feel patronizing to him.
- **But competitive in the right way:** Would care if he could see "I flagged this 3 days before it got banned."

## His actual fraud-hunting workflow
1. Opens Telegram job channels (5-6 he monitors)
2. Skans rapidly — he can spot a scam headline in 2 seconds
3. For suspicious ones: reads the full post, checks the poster's profile age, looks for duplicate text across channels
4. Reports to channel admins (rarely responds), sometimes posts to his anti-fraud group
5. Session: 30-45 minutes, 2-4x per week

He tried telejobs in March, sorted by sus descending. Read 20+ cards in one session. Came back twice. Then stopped — the card-by-card reading was slower than his own scanning, and there was no feedback on whether his reports did anything.

## What he'd find compelling about Scam Guessr (hypothesis)
- "I disagree" button with reason capture: this is the feature for him. It turns his expertise into a data point.
- Seeing his disagreement accuracy tracked over time: "you were right 78% of the time"
- A cluster/map view where he can spot coordinated rings — 5 faces that look similar = same operation
- Impact signal: "3 postings you flagged were removed this week"
- NOT the card-flip reveal game — that's Аня's thing, too slow for him

## What might NOT work
- The game framing entirely: "Scam Guessr" sounds like it's for teenagers
- Having to play the card game to access the map/cluster view
- Losing his findings when the session ends — needs persistence
- Leaderboards: doesn't care about global rank vs. strangers

## Critical tension
Олег and Аня want fundamentally different things. Аня wants entertainment with a fraud signal as a hook. Олег wants a fraud tool with better UX. **A single product may not serve both well** — the game framing that attracts Аня may actively repel Олег.
