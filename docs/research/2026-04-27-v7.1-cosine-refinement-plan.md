Branch from `glasses_slider_v7_000001200.safetensors`. Goal: fix two
specific failure modes of v7 step 1200 without losing what works.

## What's wrong with 1200

1. **Dead zone at s∈[0, 0.75].** Fraction-with-glasses is flat at 0
   until s=1.0 in-dist (and 0.75 held-out at 0.11). The slider only
   does anything above s≈1.0. Spearman penalty comes entirely from this
   flat tail, not from non-monotonicity in the engaged range.
2. **20/189 identity cells <0.4.** Mean is fine (0.712); the floor is
   not. Likely concentrated at high strength on the demographics
   that struggle (latino_m, young_black_m, hijab woman) — held-out
   scenes outside the studio-lit training distribution.
3. **Held-out demographic dropouts.** latino_m_cafe and
   young_black_m_bench are near-flat across the strength sweep.
   Likely scene-confound (cafe/bench backgrounds vs studio training).

## Hypothesis: the spatial mask is now over-concentrated

The mask was a gradient-budget allocator for the cold-start phase —
v5/v6 falsified the optimizer-dynamics story; v7's `eye_mask_peak=5.0`
shifted ~3.5× of the loss-mass to the eye region and broke through.
Once engagement is locked in (which 1200 confirms), the same mask peak
keeps paying cost without buying anything new:

- Narrow loss support → high-amplitude "spike" direction. The slider
  must be pushed past s=1.0 to register, instead of producing a smooth
  amplitude curve from 0.
- Loss-mass concentrated in ~5% of the image → over-commitment in
  that region distorts surrounding face shape on demographics where
  the eye-region prior is weakest. Predicts the 20 sub-0.4 identity
  cells are clustered on minority demographics + held-out scenes.

For refinement we want to **broaden the support, not sharpen it**.

Counter-hypothesis to falsify: if v7.1 with broader mask shows
*increased* bundle without fixing the dead zone, the diagnosis is
wrong — the dead zone is a property of the LoRA's effective rank /
capacity allocation, not the loss support — and v7.2 should keep
mask=2.5 but raise rank or adjust α.

## Plan

Branch from 1200 with three deltas vs v7:

1. **`eye_mask_peak: 5.0 → 2.5`.** Halve the loss-mass concentration.
   Keep σ_y=0.06, σ_x=0.18 unchanged — same eye-region focus, less
   peakedness. This is the only structural change.
2. **`lr_scheduler: constant → cosine_with_min_lr`,
   `lr: 1.25e-4 → 2e-5`, `lr_min: 5e-6`.** Refinement schedule. Spend
   the budget on spreading the existing learned direction, not on
   carving deeper.
3. **`steps: 2500 → 400`.** Shorter run; cosine over 400 steps. v7
   plateaued by 1200 → 1300; 400 more refinement steps is the right
   order of magnitude.

Hold everything else at v7 known-good: α=1, r=16, η=4, adamw8bit, v2
dataset, EMA off, xattn scope, anchor=null, sample_every=50.

`save_every: 50` keeps the index workflow reusable. The numerical
index script (`v7_checkpoint_index.py`) and eval battery
(`measure_slider --strengths 0.0 0.25 ... 1.75`) work unchanged on
v7.1 outputs.

## Acceptance criteria for v7.1

Run the same eval battery on the final v7.1 checkpoint (and 2–3
earlier ones to find the new sweet spot). Pass = all of:

- **Dead zone shrinks**: fraction-with-glasses at s=0.75 in-dist
  ≥ 0.33 (one-third of cells engage by 0.75, vs 0 at v7-1200).
- **Identity floor lifts**: ≤ 5 cells with cos<0.4 (vs 20 at v7-1200).
  Equivalently: min cos over the 189 cells > 0.3.
- **Engagement preserved**: fraction-with-glasses at s=1.5 in-dist
  ≥ 0.78 (vs 0.89; allow some degradation if bundle drops).
- **Bundle does not creep**: bundle_pos in the index ≤ 0.10 (vs 0.081).

Soft target: in-dist Spearman ρ over s∈[0, 1.75] ≥ 0.85 (vs 0.669).
The mechanism for raising ρ is fixing the dead zone — engaged-range
ranking is fine.

If dead zone shrinks but bundle creeps past 0.12, the mask was load-
bearing for bundle suppression at 5.0 and we should retreat to peak=4
for v7.2. If dead zone does not shrink, the rank-capacity hypothesis
above takes over and v7.2 should test r=32 at peak=2.5.
